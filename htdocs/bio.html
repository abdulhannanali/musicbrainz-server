      Robert Kaye [ homepage | blog ]

Robert is a die hard MP3 fan dedicated to improving the digital audio experience. After studying Computer Engineering at Cal Poly he joined Xing Technology where he was part of the MP3 team and served as the development manager for the AudioCatalyst project. While at EMusic, Robert has been working on the Zinf (formerly FreeAmp) and Obsequeiuem projects, besides working on MusicBrainz. Robert is currently self-employed doing contract pogramming work here and there.   Hire me!

If you like the work that Robert has done and would like to help him continue doing his work, please consider contributing to his personal contribution box.

 
      Johan Pouwelse

Johan is the owner of the MP3.nl web site that is home to an extensive music database. With extensive UNIX experience, client/server programming and compression technology he developed several programs to use MP3 technology in new ways. His current project is adding MTV like Info Facts and Say What functionality to MP3 files.

A firm believer in the GNU Public License, he publishes the source code of all his work. Currently he is working on his Ph.D. at the Technical University of Delft and applying audio compression in his research. Obtained a Cum Laude masters degree after only two years of study at the same university.

 
     
David Evans [ home | profile ]

After a BSc in mathematics at Southampton University, David first worked for Watford Electronics for a year before moving on to CSC, where he coded in C against Ingres, Informix and Oracle databases.  After three years there, David went to work for Transatlantic Software, where he gained experience in SQL Server and UNIX before working on database-driven web sites written in Perl.  Since December 2000 David has worked for Power Internet where he specialises in implementing web sites using Apache and mod_perl on Linux.

Although somewhat rusty through lack of practice, David can still play the violin.
